Item 2. Management's Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations

The following discussion should be read in conjunction with the condensed consolidated financial statements and accompanying notes included in Part I, Item 1 of this Form 10-Q.

Net sales increased 5% or $4.4 billion during the third quarter of 2024 compared to the same quarter in 2023. iPhone net sales increased 3% year-over-year, reflecting continued strong demand for iPhone 15 models. Services net sales increased 14% year-over-year, driven by growth across all geographic segments and service offerings including the App Store, Apple Music, and iCloud.

Gross margin increased to 46.3% during the third quarter of 2024 compared to 44.5% during the same quarter in 2023, primarily reflecting a more favorable sales mix and favorable adjustments to prior period estimated variable consideration, partially offset by the impact of foreign exchange rates and pricing actions in certain countries and regions.

Operating expenses increased 8% year-over-year primarily due to increased headcount-related expenses and higher spending on research and development initiatives related to artificial intelligence and machine learning capabilities.

Item 3. Quantitative and Qualitative Disclosures About Market Risk

The Company is exposed to market risk from changes in certain commodity prices, foreign currency exchange rates, interest rates, and equity prices. The Company regularly reviews its exposure to fluctuations in interest rates and uses derivative financial instruments to hedge some of its exposure.

Foreign Currency Risk. In general, the Company is a net receiver of currencies other than the U.S. dollar. Accordingly, changes in exchange rates, and in particular a strengthening of the U.S. dollar, will negatively affect the Company's net sales and gross margins as expressed in U.S. dollars. The Company uses derivative instruments designated as cash flow hedges to hedge its exposure to foreign exchange risk.

PART II

Item 1. Legal Proceedings

The Company is subject to legal proceedings and claims that have arisen in the ordinary course of business and have not been fully resolved. The outcome of litigation is inherently uncertain. If one or more legal matters were resolved against the Company in a reporting period for amounts in excess of management's expectations, the Company's financial condition and operating results for that reporting period could be materially adversely affected.

Epic Games, Inc. v. Apple Inc. In August 2020, Epic Games filed a lawsuit against the Company alleging that the Company engaged in anticompetitive behavior in violation of U.S. federal antitrust law and California state law. The case went to trial and a final judgment was entered by the trial court. The Company and Epic have each appealed portions of the judgment, and those appeals remain pending.

The Company is also subject to various government investigations and regulatory inquiries. In June 2023, the European Commission opened a formal investigation into the Company under the Digital Markets Act related to the Company's App Store rules and practices. The Company is cooperating with this investigation.

Item 1A. Risk Factors

The Company's business is subject to various risks and uncertainties. There have been no material changes to the risk factors previously disclosed in Part I, Item 1A of the Company's Annual Report on Form 10-K for the fiscal year ended September 30, 2023, as filed with the SEC on November 3, 2023, other than as described below.

Risks related to artificial intelligence technologies. The Company has been investing in and deploying new AI-powered features and services. AI technologies present new risks including risks related to data privacy, algorithmic bias, errors in AI-generated outputs, and evolving regulatory requirements. The Company faces competition from other companies that are also rapidly developing and deploying AI capabilities, some of which may have greater resources. Failure to develop or deploy AI technologies effectively could adversely affect the Company's competitive position and results of operations.
